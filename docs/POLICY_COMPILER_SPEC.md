# Policy Compiler Specification

## Doctrine

dot.ink owns what must be proven.
The customer owns what they prefer.

The compiler derives necessity, never preference.

## Boundary

The compiler enforces the objective governance floor.
Customer policy is a subjective overlay above that floor.

The compiler may derive only what is required for:
- authority
- bounded execution
- readback
- ambiguity handling
- receipts and replay

It may not invent:
- business thresholds beyond the objective minimum
- customer segmentation rules
- exception posture
- subjective operational taste

## Compile Profiles

### proof_minimum (default)

- objective-governance only
- customer extensions rejected
- minimal lawful compile target
- intended for provable floor generation

### extended

- allows customer policy overlays above the floor
- does not weaken or replace objective invariants
- intended for explicit customer-authored policy layers

## Mandatory compiler behavior

The compiler MUST:
- parse hostile input strictly
- normalize deterministically
- reject ambiguous authority
- reject missing objective conditions
- bind compiled output to truth-owner surfaces
- emit deterministic policy artifacts
- epoch-pin compiled results

The compiler MUST NOT:
- infer company intent
- guess business rules
- convert preference into objective law
- weaken global invariants

## Failure rule

Missing objective truth -> FAIL
Missing subjective preference -> OK

## One-line summary

We compile the provable floor, not the opinionated ceiling.
